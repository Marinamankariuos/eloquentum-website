Privacy Policy

How Eloquentum handles your data.

This policy explains what Eloquentum collects, how the app uses AI and speech providers, how English Challenge works, and how you can control or delete your data.

Last updated: June 16, 2026 No sale of personal data Tracking: no iOS app + English Challenge

At a glance

Eloquentum is a diagnostic-first English learning app. We use account, diagnostic, practice, progress, subscription, speech, and challenge data to provide the product, personalize practice, protect the service, and support users.

No sale of personal data. Eloquentum does not sell personal data or share it for cross-context behavioral advertising. The in-app Do Not Sell My Data control also suppresses analytics as a defense-in-depth privacy control.

Apple requires app developers to keep privacy disclosures accurate and publicly accessible. This page is intended to match the current in-app Privacy Policy, App Privacy worksheet, and iOS privacy manifest.

App Store Privacy Label Basis

The current iOS privacy manifest and App Store privacy worksheet identify these linked, non-tracking data categories when the corresponding features are used:

  • Email address, name, and user ID for account, support, entitlement, and challenge records.
  • Audio data for voice features, transcription, and optional Speech Coach pronunciation assessment.
  • Sensitive information for English Challenge integrity signals where Teams-admin challenge paths are enabled.
  • Product interaction, purchase history, other user content, crash data, other usage data, and performance data for app functionality, consented analytics, diagnostics, subscriptions, learning records, and support.

The app does not declare tracking, advertising-data use, camera, photo library, location, contacts, or health data in the current release basis.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the information needed to create and run your Eloquentum account: name, email address, profile settings, native language, target level, goals, selected Practice Focus, subscription status, and support messages you send us.

At signup, we store a minimal record that you accepted the current Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, including version, timestamp, source, and account id.

When you use the app, we collect diagnostic answers, practice responses, timing, accuracy, saved review items, progress evidence, and AI feedback records. These records power your diagnostic profile, Daily Session, review plan, and progress screens.

When you record speech, we may process audio, transcripts, timing, fluency signals, and speech/transcription confidence signals. When you take an English Challenge, we also collect challenge link/session metadata, section timing, responses, integrity telemetry, consent flags, and review/export audit records.

2. How We Use Data

We use your data to provide the product you chose: diagnostics, personalized practice, feedback, review scheduling, progress reports, account support, subscription access, and security controls.

Practice Focus selections, including IELTS® or TOEFL®, are used internally for personalization. Eloquentum does not share Practice Focus data with IELTS, ETS, TOEFL, or official test providers unless a future feature asks for separate explicit consent.

Some processing is necessary to provide the service. Some processing, such as optional AI providers, analytics, push notifications, and English Challenge submission, requires separate consent or an explicit user action.

You can manage AI and analytics controls in Profile -> Privacy & Data -> AI Features. Notification controls live in Profile -> Display & Sound.

3. AI Providers and Controls

Eloquentum uses Gemini as the primary text-AI provider. OpenAI is used only when configured and consented as fallback/secondary text AI, for English Challenge secondary scoring where configured, and for Whisper-1 transcription when server transcription is needed.

Azure Speech may be used only for the optional Speech Coach add-on, or for future known-reference/read-aloud challenge pronunciation evidence after required policy, legal, provider, benchmark, entitlement, and consent gates. Current open-ended English Challenge speaking answers are not scored against the taker's transcript as reference text.

  • Gemini - exercise generation, feedback, Mate conversation, reporting assistance, and text-based scoring support.
  • OpenAI - fallback text AI when enabled, plus secondary scoring for English Challenge writing and speaking audit trails when configured.
  • OpenAI Whisper-1 - speech-to-text transcription when server transcription is needed.
  • Azure Speech - optional provider-grade pronunciation assessment for Speech Coach known-target clips, or future known-reference/read-aloud challenge pronunciation evidence after required gates.

Data sent through configured API providers is not used by those providers to train third-party foundation models for other customers under their API/data-processing terms. Provider names and models may change over time, but Eloquentum must keep the provider layer explicit, consented, and easy to update. If a new sub-processor is added, we will disclose it and request any required consent before sending your data to that provider.

AI feedback and scores are practice estimates unless explicitly marked otherwise. They are not official IELTS®, TOEFL®, immigration, academic, employment, certification, or government credentials, and they do not guarantee any outcome. Mate is an AI speaking partner, not a human tutor or examiner.

AI systems can make mistakes. If you see incorrect, offensive, or unsafe content, contact support@eloquentum.com and include the screen, exercise, or challenge context so we can investigate.

4. Speech, Audio, and Biometrics

Microphone access is used only when you start a speaking feature. Audio may be processed locally when supported, sent for server transcription when a feature needs a reliable transcript, or sent to the optional Speech Coach pronunciation provider when the add-on, provider consent, known target text, supported audio format, and minute-cap checks pass.

On web browsers, optional live captions may use the browser's built-in speech service, which can send microphone audio to the browser vendor's speech-recognition service.

Eloquentum stores the transcript, timing, provider evidence, scores, and practice evidence needed to show feedback and progress. Eloquentum does not retain raw consumer practice audio after transcription/scoring completes. Providers may retain submissions briefly for abuse monitoring under their API/data-processing terms; they do not use your audio to train their models.

No voiceprints. Eloquentum does not extract, derive, or retain voiceprints, speaker embeddings, speaker verification templates, voice clones, or biometric identifiers intended to identify you from your voice. Speech is used for language practice, transcription, scoring support, and integrity signals where disclosed.

5. English Challenge

English Challenge is a low-stakes learning, placement, and invitation surface issued by a person, family, or Teams organization. It is advisory only: not a verified exam, official certification, employment proof, automatic decision, or learning path. You must accept the pre-challenge consent flags before starting.

Challenge data may include your email or recipient identifier, answers, transcripts, timing, section completion, integrity signals, AI scoring outputs, human-review status, export status, and audit metadata. For Teams-admin challenges, integrity signals may include typing-rhythm summaries such as dwell-time and paste-attempt statistics; personal and family challenges do not use typing-rhythm analysis.

Productive scoring uses Gemini as the primary provider and OpenAI as the secondary provider when configured. Eloquentum stores raw scores, divergence, selected higher valid score, and model metadata for auditability.

Family owners can see each member's English Challenge allowance. Per-member challenge-sending activity counts are shown to the owner only when that family member has enabled family data sharing.

Challenge results are advisory information for the sender or sponsor. Eloquentum does not make admission, placement, employment, or other organization decisions. Organization exports require human-review attestation in the product. Sponsors are responsible for lawful use, notices, accommodations, privacy controls, and any decisions they make.

Raw challenge responses are retained only for the configured limited review window, currently 24 hours in the active challenge flow. Derived scores, consent records, human-review records, integrity summaries, and export/audit metadata may be retained as needed for sponsor reporting, fraud/security review, contract performance, and legal defense.

After completing an English Challenge, you may choose to create or continue a personal Eloquentum account. Only derived learning signals are carried into the learning app. Your raw challenge answers are not used as a coaching transcript.

6. Progress, Exports, and Exam Prep

Progress screens and PDF exports are evidence surfaces. Scan results, practice mastery, review due items, exam-prep progress, and mock-test evidence should come from stored scan or practice records. We do not provide official exam registration, official test content, official exam scores, certification, immigration, academic, employment, or guaranteed outcome claims.

Practice Focus can be changed in Profile. IELTS® and TOEFL® are supported as learner-selected practice focus options only. Eloquentum is independent and is not affiliated with IELTS, ETS, TOEFL, or official test providers. Switching focus does not delete prior IELTS® or TOEFL® practice history.

7. Subscriptions and Billing

Consumer subscriptions, self-serve Teams learning-seat subscriptions, and English Challenge attempt add-ons are handled through Apple in the iOS app. We receive subscription status, attempt-pack transaction references, and receipt validation records needed to unlock access or grant attempts, but we do not store your full payment card information.

New accounts start with 7 days of Pro access through Eloquentum's server-side trial. Eloquentum does not offer a separate no-cost subscription plan. Family members inherit access from the family owner, Teams members inherit access from their workspace, and English Challenge attempts are one-time add-ons outside the subscription trial.

Current in-app fallback pricing is: Student Pro $9.99/mo or $99.99/yr; Pro $14.99/mo or $149.99/yr; Family $39.99/mo or $399.99/yr; Teams Education $9.99/seat/mo or $99.99/seat/yr, 5-seat minimum; Teams Business $12.99/seat/mo or $129.99/seat/yr, 5-seat minimum; Speech Coach $4.99/mo add-on with 150 minutes/month; English Challenge attempts $1.99 each, with packs priced at the same per-attempt rate for every tier. Checkout, App Store, or contract terms control final price, eligibility, taxes, renewal, cancellation, and refunds.

8. Analytics, Crash Reports, and Push

PostHog analytics is opt-in/consent controlled and uses pseudonymous product events. Sentry may receive pseudonymous crash/error data so we can fix defects. The Do Not Sell My Data control suppresses analytics transmission.

If you grant push permission, Apple Push Notification service may receive a device token and notification payload so iOS can deliver reminders or Teams admin alerts. We avoid putting sensitive personal information into push notification content.

9. Third-Party Processors

We use the following processors to operate the current product:

  • Supabase - authentication, database, Edge Functions, storage, and server infrastructure.
  • Google Gemini - primary text-AI processing.
  • OpenAI - configured fallback/secondary text AI and Whisper-1 transcription.
  • Microsoft Azure Speech - optional Speech Coach pronunciation assessment when enabled, consented, and entitlement-gated.
  • Apple - App Store subscriptions, StoreKit status, consumable English Challenge attempt purchases, and push notification delivery.
  • Resend - transactional email delivery where configured.
  • PostHog - consented product analytics.
  • Sentry - crash and error monitoring.

Processors may process data in the United States or other regions where they operate. For EEA, UK, and Swiss users, we rely on appropriate transfer safeguards where required, such as Standard Contractual Clauses, provider transfer frameworks, or explicit consent where legally appropriate.

10. Retention and Deletion

We retain account, subscription, diagnostic, practice, review, and progress data while your account is active so the app can preserve your roadmap and history. You can delete your account from Profile or contact privacy@eloquentum.com.

Account deletion removes active learning data unless we must retain limited records for security, fraud prevention, payment/accounting, App Store receipt and trial anti-abuse bindings, Terms/Privacy acceptance evidence, Teams challenge audit trails, monthly family report records already generated for the owner, legal obligations, or dispute defense. Raw challenge responses follow the shorter challenge retention rule described above.

11. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, export, delete, restrict, or object to processing of your personal data. You can exercise in-app controls in Profile, or contact privacy@eloquentum.com.

California residents may request access, deletion, correction, opt out of sale/share, limit use of sensitive personal information where applicable, and exercise these rights without discrimination. Eloquentum does not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

EEA/UK users may also request restriction, portability, objection, and review of automated decisions where applicable. English Challenge recipients may request human review of an adverse organization action through the sponsor or sender that controls the decision process.

12. Children, Family, and Teams Accounts

Eloquentum is not directed to children under 13. Family accounts are owner-led and capped at 6 accounts. Family members have their own accounts and inherited access; the family owner manages billing.

Family members can see each member's name and, only when sharing is enabled, shared progress such as streak, CEFR level, recent sessions, last active date, and accuracy. Family owner monthly reports can include opted-in per-skill scores, strengths, and improvement areas.

Teams admins can see member identity and workspace details such as name, email, role, department, and job title. Learning performance fields such as CEFR, streak, weekly sessions, last active date, and progress are shown only according to the product's Teams data-sharing controls.

If you believe a child provided personal information without appropriate consent, contact privacy@eloquentum.com so we can review and delete the account if required.
